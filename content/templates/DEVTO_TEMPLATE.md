# dev.to Article Template

## Frontmatter

```
---
title: [topic title — opinion angle, not textbook title]
published: true
description: [1 sentence — what you built and what you think about it]
tags: ai, machinelearning, python, anthropic
cover_image: [leave blank — add manually]
---
```

---

## Structure

### The moment that made me want to understand this

[2–3 paragraphs. A specific moment from your work on FinMentor or BODEGA AI where you hit a limitation or had a question. Personal, specific, honest. This is what makes people read on.]

### What [concept] actually is (and what it isn't)

[The explanation — but through the lens of what you expected vs. what you found. Not a textbook definition. Your first mental model, then what was wrong about it, then what's actually true.]

### What I built

[Describe the notebook. What problem it solves, what each section does. Link to the GitHub repo. Include 1–2 code snippets — the most interesting ones, not the boilerplate.]

### What surprised me

[1–3 concrete observations. Things that weren't in the paper or the docs. What the code revealed that the theory didn't make obvious.]

### My honest take

[Your opinion. What this technique gets right. Where it falls short. What you'd want to see improved. This is the section people cite and share.]

### What's next

[1 paragraph. What you're building or studying next. Link to the repo for people to follow along.]

---

## Notes

- 800–1500 words total
- Conversational but precise — smart engineer writing to smart engineers, not a professor writing to students
- The "what surprised me" section is where the post earns its place on the internet
- The "honest take" section is what gets shared — be specific about limitations, not vague
